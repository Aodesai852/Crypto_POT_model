\vspace{1.5em}
{\centering
Figure X: Lower-Tail Dynamic Net Spillovers across Cryptocurrencies\\
}
\vspace{0.5em}
{\raggedright \footnotesize
\textit{Notes:} This figure illustrates the dynamic net risk spillovers from each coin to all other coins in the set. Net risk spillovers are computed as the difference between risk spillovers transmitted to other coins and risk spillovers received from them. Risk spillovers are estimated using a generalized vector autoregressive (VAR) framework following Diebold and Yilmaz (2012), based on forecast error variance decompositions of the reciprocal lower-tail index. The rolling-window size of the VAR model is set to 200 days, and the threshold of the univariate peaks-over-threshold (uPOT) model is set at the 95\% quantile. As part of robustness checks, we additionally construct net spillover measures using alternative thresholds at the 85\% and 90\% quantiles, as well as alternative window sizes of 150 and 250 days. The results indicate that the dynamic net spillover estimates are robust to the choice of both the window size and the threshold. The corresponding figures are available from the authors upon request.
}
